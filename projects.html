Computer Science Research Projects

1. Burning Geometric Graphs (Theoretical Computer Science, Complexity theory, Graph Theory). ▿

  August 2019 to July 2020
  Supervisor: Dr Swapnil A Lokhande, Assistant Professor, Indian Institute of Information Technology Vadodara, India.
    Studied the graph burning problem. We showed that graph burning is NP-Complete on interval graphs, permutation graphs and disc graphs. As corollary, we have that burning of trees, chordal graphs, path forests, planar graphs, bipartite graphs, spider graphs, forests is NP-Complete. For these corollaries, results are present in the literature prior to our work.
    Articles published: Burning Grids and Intervals.
    This study was conducted as part of my MTech dissertation.

2. Telemetry Device Based on Telecare Medical Information System (embedded system: Raspberry Pi, Arduino) (as Member at Centre for Robotics and Artificial Intelligence). ▿

  January 2017 to April 2017
  Supervisor: Mr Kamlesh Raghuvanshi, Assistant Professor, Assistant Professor, Department of Computer Science, Ramanujan College (University of Delhi).
  4 members.
    Reduced Cost Telemetry Device: takes various observations from a human body, such as pulse, temperature and sends the data over the network to a remote android smartphone.     This study was conducted as part of my MTech dissertation.
