Welcome! I am a Graduate Student at Computer Science and Engineering, Michigan State University. I also serve as a Graduate Teaching Assistant with the department. My research interests are distributed systems, graph theory, theoretical computer science, discrete mathematics. I am also serving as a member of the Graduate Teaching Assistant Program Advisory Group with the Graduate School, Michigan State University.

As part of my doctoral research, we developed a theory that describes the necessary and sufficient properties of parallel processing algorithms that make them tolerant to asynchrony. In addition, we developed asynchrony-tolerant algorithms for several problems, and also showed for various existing algorithms that they are tolerant to asynchrony.

As part of my master's research, we wrote several NP-Completeness proofs.

During my undergrad, I dealt with several projects in embedded systems and software development. Our projects encompass solutions to problems from various domains. I organized and conducted several workshops on embedded systems and software development.

My master's and doctoral research combined have produced several results where we improve an existing algorithm. Specifically, among other results, we show for several existing systems that
   (1) an algorithm would work the same with fewer assumtions (e.g., not needing a scheduler), or
   (2) an algorithm would work better as it is (e.g. converging in fewer rounds), or
   (3) a problem can be solved in lesser resources' requirement (e.g., not needing to produce additional data structure)
than originally assumed.
